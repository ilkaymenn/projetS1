Article 1 // Education and Girls

  • Feminism : doctrine that advocates equality between men and women, and the expansion of women's role in society.
  • Standard : Set of rules of conduct that are imposed on a social group.
  • Social position : Position, rank, importance that a person occupies in society.
  • Tuberculosis : infectious and contagious disease, most commonly affecting the lung.
  • Abstention : avoidance of doing something.
  • Economic conditions : situation in which a person finds himself or herself, not being able to support himself or herself financially..
  • Moral conditions : set of rules of conduct, considered absolutely good or derived from a certain conception of life.
  • Emancipation : act of freeing oneself from a bond, a hindrance, a state of dependence, a domination, a prejudice.

Article 2 // Malala Yousafzai

  • Activist :form of collective commitment to a cause of a moral, religious, social, political, associative or trade union nature, often with a view to protesting against what is perceived as an injustice.
  • Spokesperson : person who speaks on behalf of someone else, an assembly, a group.
  • Pacifism : has two possible meanings including the action of peace advocates, or a doctrine of non-violence.
  • Taliban : Member of an armed fundamentalist Islamic movement in Afghanistan.
  • Breach : Not respecting (a commitment, a law).
  • Paralysed : Complete reduction or deprivation of the ability to move, to feel.
  • To militate : To militate for, against... to constitute a reason, an argument (for or against).
  • Contributor : Person, group who takes part in the realisation of a project, an enterprise.
  • Pashtun : Iranian people divided into several large tribes and speaking mainly Pashto.
  • Malalai of Maiwand : national folk heroine from Afghanistan who rallied the Pashtun army against British troops in 1880 at the Battle of Maiwand.

Article 3 // Girl Rising

  • CorrElation : link, reciprocal relationship.
  • Eradication : stop, remove, heal.
  • Misery : state of extreme poverty, destitution.
  • Socio-economic impetus : movement relating to social problems in their relation to economic problems.
  • Emerging countries : countries whose economic situation is developing. This growth is calculated according to GDP, new businesses and infrastructure, and the standard of living and quality of life of the inhabitants.
  • Faith : absolute trust in someone or something.
  • Developing country : country moving from a state of chronic underdevelopment to the process of development (economic and social transformation induced by a growth rate; expansion).
  • Non-profit organisation : non-profit organisation is a grouping of two or more people who decide to pool resources in order to carry out an activity with a primary purpose other than their personal enrichment. The disinterested nature of the activity therefore prohibits the distribution of a profit to the partners.
  • Early pregnancies : concerns pregnant girls whose young age increases the risk of health effects and negative social consequences.
